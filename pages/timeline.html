ALGALAIDE
Manifesto Timeline Parliament Community Characters Remedies Theories

TIMELINE

Late 2024

Marine Heatwave Conditions Form

Sea surface temperatures across South Australia rise well above long-term averages. Scientists later identify these conditions as a key precursor for harmful algal blooms.

Mid–Late March 2025

First Mass Marine Deaths Observed

Fish, rays, sharks, and dolphins begin washing ashore along the Fleurieu Peninsula. Beach foam, water discolouration, and respiratory irritation are reported.

The species Karenia mikimotoi is identified in samples.

April 2025

Public Alarm, Official Silence

Community members, fishers, surfers, and divers raise concerns publicly. Government communication remains minimal.

May 2025

First Mentions in Parliament

The algal bloom is first raised formally in the South Australian Parliament by Tammy Franks MLC and Robert Simms MLC.

“This is not a hypothetical threat — it is unfolding now.”
June 2025

Bloom Reaches Metropolitan Beaches

Storm activity pushes bloom-affected water into Gulf St Vincent. Adelaide beaches are visibly impacted.

Mid–Late 2025

Political Fracture & Downplaying

Government figures dispute language describing the bloom as “toxic”. Public messaging focuses on reassurance and tourism optics.

Late 2025

Claims of Delayed Investigation

A senior scientist later alleges she was instructed to pause investigation into causes of the bloom until after the state election.

January 2026

Scientist Resigns

The scientist resigns from advisory roles, citing an adversarial environment and political interference.

Election Year

The Bloom Becomes Political

The algal bloom remains unresolved as South Australia enters a state election cycle. Accountability becomes a campaign issue.
