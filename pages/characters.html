Manifesto Timeline Parliament Community Characters

CHARACTERS

This crisis is not just chemical or biological — it is human. The algal bloom revealed a cast of recurring personalities that appear whenever environmental collapse collides with politics, money, fear, ego, and hope. Understanding these characters — how they collaborate, obstruct, amplify, or neutralise one another — is essential if we are to navigate forward rather than repeat the same disaster in a different costume.

DR ALGIE

The Bloom Itself / Comic Anti-Hero

Dr Algie is the algal bloom personified — not as a monster, but as a smug, semi-sentient force that thrives on human delay and denial. Half mad scientist, half accidental god, Dr Algie does not see itself as evil. It simply responds to the conditions provided.

This character exists to remind us that the bloom is not a villain with intent. It is an outcome. It flourishes when nutrients, warming waters, political inertia, and industrial runoff align just right.

TIN FOIL HATMAN

The Misdirector / Chaos Engine

Tin Foil Hatman embodies the conspiratorial response to crisis — loud, confident, angry, and catastrophically wrong. Chinese submarines. Desalination plants. Lighting tricks. Anything except the boring, uncomfortable truth.

This archetype includes political figures who actively downplayed or undermined the severity of the bloom early on, muddying public discourse and giving misinformation institutional legitimacy.

SHARKFACE

Capital / Corporate Power

Sharkface is capitalism without camouflage — a shark-headed businessman clutching cash and a cigar, amused by environmental destruction so long as profits remain intact.

This character represents corporate fishing interests, development lobbies, energy giants, and the quiet economic pressures exerted on parliament. Indigenous rights, small communities, and ecosystems are collateral.

COUNCILLOR JENNY

Bureaucracy / Local Government

Councillor Jenny represents the public servant — overworked, underpowered, risk-averse, and structurally encouraged to defer responsibility upward until it evaporates.

This archetype includes officials who later claimed they were instructed to pause investigations. Not villains, but conduits through which inaction quietly becomes policy.

DOUGIE B

The Charismatic Bridge

Dougie B is where culture meets politics. Part celebrity, part activist, part fallen or rising politician. Charismatic, relatable, and capable of making complex issues feel human — and cool.

This archetype includes community figures, outspoken Greens MLCs, disgraced politicians attempting re-entry through ecological advocacy, and grassroots documentarians whose visibility forces public attention.

THE SOCIETY OF SNOTTY SCIENTISTS

Gatekeepers of Knowledge

An elite scientific class — well funded, politically adjacent, and often hostile to dissent. Fierce internal rivalries coexist with a united front when public narratives are threatened.

This archetype reflects how power, sexism, racism, ageism, and academic hierarchy shape what science is allowed to say — and what it is not.

MARINE CELINE

The Dissenter Scientist

Marine Celine represents scientists who refuse to stay silent. Public-facing, empathetic, and often ostracised by the elite for engaging communities directly.

These figures balance integrity with ambition, activism with expertise, and often become lightning rods for both hope and resentment.

PREMIER VINNY SPEAKS

The Polished Puppet

Premier Vinny Speaks is the smiling face of state power — charismatic, disciplined, and deeply entangled with commercial interests.

This archetype represents leaders who speak of responsibility while legislating against protest, delaying investigations, and protecting donors. Not cartoon villains — far more dangerous than that.
